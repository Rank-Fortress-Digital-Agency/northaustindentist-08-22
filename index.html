WISDOM TEETH REMOVAL: HOW TO PREPARE, WHAT TO EXPECT, AND RECOVERING


Wisdom teeth removal is one of the most common dental surgeries. It removes wisdom teeth that are impacted or not coming in correctly. If you are scheduled for wisdom teeth removal, preparing yourself for what to expect is essential. In this blog post, we will discuss the surgery itself and how to recover from it. We will also provide tips on making the process a bit easier.

What Are Wisdom Teeth, And Why Do They Need To Be removed?


Wisdom teeth are the third and final set of molars most people get in their late teens or early twenties. They’re called wisdom teeth because they come in when you’re supposed to be getting wiser. Unfortunately, they don’t always come in smoothly. Impacted wisdom teeth get stuck and can’t break through the gum. They can crowd other teeth and cause pain. That’s why wisdom teeth are often removed.

If your wisdom teeth are coming in and they’re not causing any problems, you may not need to have them removed. However, if they’re impacted or causing pain, your dentist or oral surgeon will likely recommend removing them. The good news is that wisdom teeth removal is a pretty standard procedure, so you’re in good hands. It is usually an outpatient procedure, which means you can go home the same day.

How Do You Prepare For Wisdom Teeth Removal Surgery?


You will need to consult your oral surgeon to see if you need to do anything special to prepare for your surgery. If you are taking any medications, let the office staff know so they can advise you accordingly. Generally, it is recommended that patients eat a light meal before their surgery and avoid eating or drinking anything after midnight the night before. This is to prevent you from vomiting during or after your surgery.

The staff will also give you specific instructions on brushing and flossing your teeth the night before and the morning of surgery. This is important because it will help reduce the risk of infection.

On the morning of your surgery, wear comfortable clothing and take any valuables or jewelry with you. You will also want to have a friend or family member drive you home after your procedure. When you arrive at the office, the staff will go over the final details of your surgery and answer any last-minute questions you may have.

What Happens During The Surgery Itself?


The surgery is usually done under local anesthesia, which numbs the area around your wisdom teeth. You’ll be awake during the procedure but shouldn’t feel any pain. Your dentist or oral surgeon will make minor cuts in your gums to expose your wisdom teeth. Then, they’ll remove the teeth by breaking them into pieces. In some cases, they may need to remove the teeth in sections. After removing your wisdom teeth, your dentist will clean the area and close the incisions with stitches. The whole procedure usually takes about an hour.

It is normal to feel discomfort after the surgery, but your dentist will prescribe pain medication to help you manage it. You should also expect some swelling and bruising around your mouth, jaw, and cheeks. These side effects usually go away within a week or two. The recovery process can vary from person to person, but most people feel back to their usual selves within a few days.

What Can You Expect In Terms Of Pain And Discomfort After The Surgery Is Completed?


The good news is that most people report very little pain and discomfort after their surgery is completed. Most people say the worst part was the numbness caused by the local anesthetic! However, it’s important to remember that everyone experiences pain differently, so while some people may have only minor discomfort, others may require pain medication for a few days. The important thing is to listen to your body and take things easy until you feel better.

Recovery times vary from person to person, but most people are back to their routine within a week. It’s important not to push yourself too hard after the surgery, as this can delay healing. It is also essential to avoid any hard or crunchy foods for at least a week, as these can cause the stitches to come out prematurely.

Talk to your dentist or oral surgeon if you have any questions or concerns about your wisdom teeth removal. They will be happy to answer any questions you may have and help you through the process.

What Are Some Of The Risks Associated With Wisdom Teeth Removal?


While wisdom teeth removal is generally a very safe procedure, any surgery has potential risks. The most common complications include infection, dry socket (where the blood clot that forms in the socket after surgery is dislodged), and damage to surrounding teeth. However, these complications are usually rare and can be easily treated.

Before removing your wisdom teeth, discuss all potential risks and complications with your oral surgeon. This will help you make an informed decision about whether or not to proceed with the surgery. Moreover, follow all of your surgeon’s instructions before and after the procedure to minimize the risk of complications.


The recovery process after wisdom teeth removal is usually pretty straightforward. Most people experience pain and swelling for a few days after surgery, but this can be easily managed with over-the-counter pain medication. Moreover, eating soft foods and taking things easy for the first few days while your mouth heals is essential.

Follow your surgeon’s instructions carefully and contact them if you experience unusual pain or swelling. With proper care, your recovery should be quick and easy. Wisdom teeth removal is a general procedure, but it is essential to make sure that you are fully prepared for it before proceeding. Be sure to discuss all potential risks and complications with your oral surgeon and follow their instructions carefully to minimize the risk of any problems.

How Long Does It Take To Recover From Wisdom Teeth Removal Surgery Completely?


Most people recover from wisdom teeth removal surgery within a few days. However, it is not uncommon for some people to experience pain and swell up to a week after their surgery. It is essential to take it easy and rest as much as possible. Over-the-counter pain medication can help manage any discomfort you may experience.
